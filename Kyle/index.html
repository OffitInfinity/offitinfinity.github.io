My Story

Originally when entering college I had been undecided as to my path in life. This lead me to the Lorain County Community College, a place where I could chart my course without the debt incured following said path at a four-year university. Going in I planned to decide between Art, Music, and Math/History Education, starting my first semester studying 2D Design. By the next semester this path began to change course.

By Spring I had begun parting together my first PC, and with it I began studying LCCC's Computer Maintenance and Networking program. Within the first year my computer knowledge grew significantly and plans for what would grow into my homelab would enter planning. This train would maintain speed leading into the COVID-19 pandemic where it was derailed by the ebb and flow of life.

The outbreak of the pandemic coincided with my final year of study for my Associate's Degree. As the world transitioned into lockdown, I found myself delving deeper into my professional life and transitioning from Fast Food to Technology. Seeing my time at McDonald's as having reached its peak, I moved on to a position at MCPc. From there, growing scheduling conflicts would solidify my education being placed on hold.

As work began drying up at MCPc, I would see my self return to LCCC to finish what I started. Having spent time in the industry I would get a fresh kick on learning programing and using Linux.

Having completed my Associate's Degree, I am presently working on adjacent studies in Cyber Security. Additionally, I am working on courses that will transfer on to my studies for a Bachelor's in Computer Science at Western Governer's via LCCC's University Partnership Program.
