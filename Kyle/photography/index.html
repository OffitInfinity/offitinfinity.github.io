About Me

Photography has always been one of my on/off hobbies, wherein all of my cameras have been hand-me-downs from my grandfather. Though I've shot many photos on my iPhones over the years, their capabilities never satisfied the way a real kit did. Much of my photography not shot on iPhone makes extensive use of my kit's zoom capabilities.
While I started out mostly focused around pictures documenting various trips across my high school career, I grew a bit more artistic throughout college. Across my later years I grew an intrest towards film digitization. A byproduct of digging through family photos, following the deaths of my father and grandmothers in 2024.

iPhones

iPod Touch

Not exactly my first camera, as I had started out with a few point and shot film cameras as well as my earlier flip cell phones, the iPod Touch is my first camera I still have pictures from. My first hand-me-down from my grandfather, not given as a camera but a replacement for my iPod nano, it was my primary camera throughout high school, being relegated to point and shot in my later years.

iPhone XR

My first proper iPhone, I bought it in 2019. It would be my main camera from then until I upgraded to the 12 mini in 2021. It's short time would see a few park visits as well as pictures taken at work, such as those of a toad that was regulary near our storage shed.

iPhone 12 mini

My current iPhone, also my longest used camera and phone. I bought it in 2021 expecting the mini line-up to be a one-off. This phone would be my main camera for a trip across the US East Coast and another trip to the DC area for a cousin's wedding.
